Federal Account Explorer

Discover the federal government’s bank accounts

The government has almost 2,000 bank accounts that it uses to spend money. Use this interactive tree-style visualization to explore these accounts and see how much money has been spent in FY 2017.

Contract Explorer Sunburst

Who receives federal contracts?

The federal government spends about $500 billion each year on contracts -- that's roughly the size of Sweden's economy. Learn more about contract recipients and what agencies recieve from these contracts.

Homelessness Analysis

Explore federal programs that address homelessness

On a single night in 2016, more than 500,000 people experienced homelessness in the United States. Twenty percent of these people were children. Use this case study to see how graant data illuminates the federal governments approach to addressing homelessness.

Budget Function

See how the government categorizes its purchases

Data from USAspending.gov allows you to connect two different ways the government categorizes spending: what it pays for and what it's trying to achieve with those purchases. Use this visualization to explore broad spending categories (for example, national defense) and specific examples related to those categories (for example, military personnel versus military contractors).

Competition in Contracting

How often do federal agencies compete contracts?

The federal government is required to use competition when issuing contracts for goods and services, with a few exceptions. See which federal agencies employ competitive practices most frequently with this analysis.

In 2017, the government collected $3.3 trillion

Most of the federal government's income comes from federal taxes.

Most federal income comes from contributions from individuals and small businesses. Along with federal income taxes, the government collects payroll taxes. You may recognize these taxes on your paycheck that help fund programs such as Social Security, Medicare, and Unemployment Insurance. Also, corporations pay taxes as part of doing business in the United States.

The remaining sources of government income consist of excise, estate, and other taxes and fees.

How does the amount of federal income compare to the size of the economy?

Data Provided by: Bureau of Economic Analysis

In Fiscal Year 2017, the total GDP of the U.S. was $19.5 trillion.

The government collected the equivalent of 17% of GDP.

How much is $3.3 trillion? It is equivalent to a little more than $10,000 for every individual in the U.S. ($3.3 trillion divided by the U.S. population estimate in 2017, 325.7 million U.S. Census Bureau.)

Gross Domestic Product (GDP) measures the size of the nation's economy in terms of the total value of goods and services that are produced in a year. Measuring the money collected by the government relative to GDP is a helpful indicator of the economy's capacity to sustain the programs the government provides for the people and the government's success in helping the economy grow.

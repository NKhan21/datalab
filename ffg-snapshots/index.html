<div class="chapter__primary">
<h1>In 2017, <br>the federal government<span class="chapter__big-number">collected $3.3 trillion.</span></h1>
<p>The federal government collects revenue from a variety of sources, including individual income taxes, payroll taxes, corporate income taxes, and excise taxes. It also collects revenue from services like admission to national parks and customs duties.</p>
<section class="anecdote">
<h1 class="anecdote__heading">What is $3.3 trillion equivalent to?</h1>
<div class="anecdote__panes">
<div class="anecdote__pane">
<p>The federal government brings in <strong>$105,000 of revenue per second</strong>.</p>
<img class="anecdote__illustration" src="/assets/ffg/bp/revenue/revenue-1.png" alt="">
<p>In the time it takes you to read this statement, the federal government has <strong>collected roughly $1 million</strong>.</p>
</div>
<div class="anecdote__pane">
<p>$3.3 trillion equates to <strong>68 million</strong> individuals earning roughly <strong>$49,000 per year</strong>.</p>
<img class="anecdote__illustration" src="/assets/ffg/bp/revenue/revenue-2.png" alt="">
<p>That is equal to the <strong>average salary of someone in the construction industry</strong>.</p>
<p class='source'>Source - <a href='https://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes_nat.htm' rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Bureau of Labor Statistics</a></p>
</div>
<div class="anecdote__pane">
<p>$3.3 trillion is equal to a little more than <strong>$10,000 per individual in the United States (estimated at 326.4 million)</strong>.</p>
<img class="anecdote__illustration" src="/assets/ffg/bp/revenue/revenue-3.png" alt="">
<p class='source'>Source - <a href='https://www.census.gov/popclock/' rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">U.S. Census Bureau</a></p>
</div>
<div class="anecdote__pane">
<p>$3.3 trillion equates to the average household contributing <strong>$2,200 per month</strong>.</p>
<img class="anecdote__illustration" src="/assets/ffg/bp/revenue/revenue-4.png" alt="">
<p>That is roughly equal to <strong>renting a three-bedroom apartment</strong> outside <strong>Tampa, FL</strong> ($2,199 per month).</p>
<p class='source'>Source - <a href='https://www.huduser.gov/portal/datasets/50per.html#2017' rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Department of Housing and Urban Development</a> & <a href='https://www.census.gov/popclock/' rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">U.S. Census Bureau</a></p>
</div>
<div class="anecdote__pane">
<p>$3.3 trillion equates to the <strong>revenue of the top 18 companies</strong> in the S&P 500.</p>
<img class="anecdote__illustration" src="/assets/ffg/bp/revenue/revenue-5.png" alt="">
<p class='source'>Figures based on 2017 10-K reports for S&P 500 Companies</p>
</div>
<div class="anecdote__pane">
<p>If the revenue of the federal government was split between the <strong>126 million households</strong> estimated in the United States in 2017. It would equate to <strong>$26,000 per household</strong>.</p>
<img class="anecdote__illustration" src="/assets/ffg/bp/revenue/revenue-6.png" alt="">
<p class='source'>Source - <a href='https://www.census.gov/popclock/' rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">U.S. Census Bureau</a></p>

<h1>In 2017,<br>the federal government<span class="chapter__big-number">spent $4.0 trillion.</span></h1>

<h1>In 2017,<br>the federal government</br>spent $670 billion more than it collected,<span class="chapter__big-number">resulting in a deficit.</span></h1>
<p>The difference between what the federal government collects and what it spends is referred to as a deficit or surplus. In 2017, the federal government spent more than it collected, resulting in a deficit.</p>

<section class="anecdote">

<p>In 2017, there were an estimated amount of <strong>126 million households</strong> in the United States.</p>

</div>

<div class="chapter__primary">
<h1>In 2017,<br>the public debt<span class="chapter__big-number">increased to $20.2 trillion.</span></h1>
<p>To pay for a deficit, the federal government usually takes on debt. The public debt is essentially the amount that the government currently owes from deficits and other liabilities that have accumulated over time.</p>
<p>Are debt and deficit the same thing? No, but they do affect one another.</p>

<section class="anecdote">
<h1 class="anecdote__heading">What is $20.2 trillion equivalent to?</h1>
<div class="anecdote__panes">
<div class="anecdote__pane">
<p>When the <strong>National Debt Clock in New York City</strong> was installed in 1989, the national debt was $2.7 trillion.</p>
<img class="anecdote__illustration" src="/assets/ffg/bp/debt/debt-1.png" alt="">
<p><strong>In 28 years, the national debt has grown by $17.5 trillion.</strong></p>
</div>
<div class="anecdote__pane">
<p>If the national debt was split evenly among households, <strong>your household's share</strong> would be roughly <strong>$160,000</strong>.</p>
<img class="anecdote__illustration" src="/assets/ffg/bp/debt/debt-2.png" alt="">
<p>That is about equal to the <strong>median home price in Pensacola, Fl</strong> ($161,300).</p>
<p class='source'>Source - <a href='https://www.census.gov/popclock/' rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">U.S. Census Bureau</a></p>
</div>
<div class="anecdote__pane">
<p>$20.2 trillion equates to a little more than <strong>$61,000</strong> per individual in the U.S.</p>
<img class="anecdote__illustration" src="/assets/ffg/bp/debt/debt-3.png" alt="">
<p>That is equal to the <strong>average salary of a Human Resources Specialist</strong>.</p>
<p class='source'>Source - <a href='https://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes_nat.htm' rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Bureau of Labor Statistics</a> & <a href='https://www.census.gov/popclock/' rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">U.S. Census Bureau</a></p>
</div>
